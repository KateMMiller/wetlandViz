Project Information

This website presents the results of long-term monitoring of freshwater wetlands in Acadia National Park (ACAD). The study is carried out by the Northeast Temperate Network (NETN) Inventory and Monitoring Program.  NETN began monitoring permanent freshwater wetlands in ACAD in 2011. NETN defines permanent wetlands as those that are saturated or inundated for most or all of the growing season (i.e., not vernal pools) and are dominated by hydrophytic plants. The overall goal of the freshwater wetland monitoring program is to monitor status and trends in vegetation, hydrology, soil and water quality, and indicators of wetland condition and stressors in ACAD's freshwater wetlands (Miller &; Mitchell 2013).

NETN uses a three-tiered approach to wetland monitoring, where level 1 is a GIS-based monitoring of landscape condition, level 2 is a rapid field-assessment, and level 3 is an intensive field-assessment. NETN conducts level 3 monitoring in 10 sentinel sites, which were non-randomly selected to represent the best condition of various wetland types in ACAD, and are sampled following protocols developed by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) for the National Wetland Condition Assessment (NWCA; EPA 2011a). In addition to sampling the EPA NWCA protocol in the sentinel sites, NETN monitors hourly water level during the growing season in the eight sentinel sites on Mount Desert Island (MDI), the results of which are shown in the Hydrographs panel.

During the years between the EPA NWCA, NETN conducts rapid assessment monitoring (RAM; Level 1 and 2) following the USA-Rapid Assessment Manual (USA-RAM; EPA 2011b) at 40 randomly located wetland sites in ACAD. Each site is rated using the Vegetation Multimetric Indicator (Veg. MMI; Miller et al. 2016), which rates wetland condition as Good, Fair or Poor.

The data and photopoints presented in the Map Panel and the Species List are from the most recent survey of available data. For RAM sites, the data are from 2020-2024, the second or third sample of each site. For Sentinel sites, the data are from the 2021 survey. Note that protected species have been removed from the data displayed here. Protected species data are available upon request and approval by NETN and ACAD staff to distribute. The data in the Hydrographs panel include all years that monitoring data are available.
